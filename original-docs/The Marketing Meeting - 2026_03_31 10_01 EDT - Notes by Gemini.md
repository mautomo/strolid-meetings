Michael Donovan noted that they are approaching Account-Based Marketing (ABM) pragmatically before rolling into intent data signals and lead magnets.

Michael Donovan also suggested that this strategy could be integrated into current customer messaging if a specific campaign was needed across all touch points.

They propose a seamless, zero-friction experience for prospects, such as providing access to an ROI calculator, benchmark report, or dealer BDC playbook without requiring them to re-enter their information if they are already in the Customer Relationship Management (CRM) system. This seamless process ensures that the full lineage of what drove a specific outcome is logged in the CRM.

Michael Donovan explained that the immediate steps involve content everywhere, and they require guidance on the pillar content—such as a dealer BDC playbook or a 2026 benchmark report—around which a value exchange can be built and then incorporated into a calendar.

- **Illustrating the Prospect Journey and Attracting Interest**: Joe Furnari requested that Michael Donovan walk the team through an example of a prospect's journey to understand how the demand generation efforts lead to engagement with the value pillars like the ROI calculator. The strategy involves applying reach and frequency across channels where the targets are, ensuring the introductory messaging creates awareness and relates to the proposed fair exchange downstream.

- **Focusing on Outcomes and Reducing Complexity**: Vinnie Micciche emphasized that they expect the marketing team to handle the execution and organizational details and asked what they need specifically from the leadership team. Vinnie Micciche shared a positive example of a podcast initiative where their only commitment is to "show up on a Wednesday or a Thursday". Vinnie Micciche requested that Michael Donovan provide a simple topic and value proposition for approval.

- **Establishing Alignment on Marketing Content**: Michael Donovan requested alignment on the content pillar, asking what problem a proposed item, such as a BDC playbook, would solve. Vinnie Micciche acknowledged that the team needs to align, but expressed concern that the content presented by Michael Donovan, such as the ROI calculator, did not align with what they had been sharing over previous months. Vinnie Micciche suggested that Michael Donovan create an outline for the proposed value propositions and use a simple "Yes or No" approval format to ensure alignment.

Joe Furnari suggested Michael Donovan use the recently gathered feedback to modify their recommendations.
