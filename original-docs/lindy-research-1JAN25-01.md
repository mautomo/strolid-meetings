COMPREHENSIVE RESEARCH REPORT: US AUTOMOTIVE INDUSTRY & FRANCHISE NEW CAR DEALERS

2025-2026 Market Analysis

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The US automotive industry is navigating a critical inflection point heading into 2026\. While franchise new car dealers remain resilient, they face unprecedented pressures from rising inventory levels, weakening consumer confidence, direct-to-consumer threats, and a fundamental shift in powertrain preferences. The market is characterized by cautious optimism tempered by significant structural challenges.

1\. SALES PERFORMANCE & MARKET CONDITIONS

Current Market Sentiment \- Dealer Confidence Declining

According to the Q4 2025 Cox Automotive Dealer Sentiment Index (CADSI), dealer confidence has sharply deteriorated:

Current Market Index: 38 (down from 43 in Q3) \- well below the positive threshold of 50

Future Outlook Index: 42 (down from 46 in Q3) \- also below positive threshold

Franchised dealers: 47 (higher than independents at 35, but both declining)

Key Quote from Cox Automotive Deputy Chief Economist Mark Strand:

"Dealers are signaling caution as 2025 ends. Persistent economic uncertainty and fading consumer confidence are weighing on sentiment. Compared to the rest of the year, the current market feels like it's running out of gas."

Customer Traffic Hits Record Lows

Overall traffic: 31 (all-time low)

In-person traffic: 29 (all-time low for franchised dealers)

Digital traffic: 40

Franchised dealers experienced the steepest declines

Sales Environment Weakening

New-vehicle sales sentiment: 49 (below 50 threshold)

Used-vehicle sales sentiment: 42 (below 50 threshold)

Franchised dealers: new-vehicle sales at 49, used-vehicle at 53

Independent dealers: used-vehicle sales at 39 (significantly weaker)

Profitability Under Pressure

Overall profit sentiment: 36 (significant decline)

Franchised dealers: 44

Independent dealers: 33

Root causes: Rising costs, margin compression, softer demand

2\. FUTURE PROJECTIONS & MARKET OUTLOOK FOR 2026

Cautious Optimism with Conditions

VADA President Don Hall's Assessment:

"Dealers are accustomed to adapting and remain focused on improving performance year over year, even amid uncertainty."

Key 2026 Projections:

Interest Rate Relief Expected: If material interest-rate relief materializes and consumer confidence rebounds, market momentum could recover in H1 2026

Fleet Sales Driving Performance: Fleet sales (government and business purchases) have been a massive support for overall numbers, with retail demand remaining steady

Inventory Normalization: New-vehicle inventory approaching pre-pandemic levels, with millions of vehicles on dealer lots nationwide

Economic Headwinds: Macro uncertainty continues to weigh on consumer confidence and purchasing power

3\. SALES, CONSUMER CONFIDENCE & MARKET TRENDS

Consumer Confidence Metrics

Declining: Economic sentiment index dropped to 39 from 43 in Q3

Independent dealers more concerned: Economic sentiment at 37 vs. franchised at 44

Rising costs and higher prices are dampening demand

Fleet Sales as Market Support

Fleet sales (government and business purchases) have been critical to overall market performance

Retail demand has remained steady but not robust

This dynamic creates vulnerability if fleet demand softens

Inventory Challenges Creating Financial Pressure

Don Hall (VADA):

"New-vehicle supply continues to rise, approaching pre-pandemic levels, with millions of vehicles on dealer lots nationwide. Higher inventory creates financial pressure for dealers due to ownership costs, floorplan expenses, and aging units, while incentives have not kept pace with rising supply."

New-vehicle inventory index: 59 (growing)

Used inventory: 43 (tight, but independents at 41 facing tighter conditions)

Aging inventory creating carrying cost pressures

4\. TECHNOLOGY SHIFTS & EMERGING TRENDS

AI Adoption in Car Shopping \- Significant but Not Dominant

Cars.com Survey (November 2025):

44% of consumers use AI tools for vehicle research

97% of AI users say technology will influence future purchase decisions

73% of AI users report AI saves time through targeted search results

66% express interest in personalized AI shopping assistants

Critical Finding: Dealerships Remain Essential

Key Quote:

"Although AI is becoming an important tool in the research phase, shoppers still want human expertise and verified data before committing to a major purchase."

Consumer Trust Levels:

71% have at least some trust in AI-generated vehicle information

63% voiced concern that recommendations may be biased

41% move to dealer/manufacturer websites once choices narrow

Most respondents still rely on dealerships for financing and final purchase decisions

Engagement Metrics

Users with AI-assisted search spend more time researching and save more listings than traditional shoppers

Cars.com adding new features: AI summaries and comparison tools

8\. COUNTER-POSITIONING & LEGACY VENDOR ATTACKS

Direct-to-Consumer Threat \- The Existential Challenge

Scout Motors (Volkswagen-backed) \- Colorado Dealer License Approval:

Scout Motors granted Colorado dealer license for direct EV sales

Puts franchise dealers nationwide on high alert

Represents broader OEM strategy to reduce dealer role

Don Hall (VADA) Assessment:

"Direct sales initiatives are not isolated experiments but part of manufacturers' broader strategies to reduce the role of franchise dealers. Many dealers underestimate the threat."

Public Sentiment Favoring Direct Sales

Growing consumer preference for buying directly from manufacturers

Perception of eliminating "middleman"

Convenience and transparency arguments

Dealer Counter-Strategy

Hall's Recommendations:

Invest in people \- "The most important asset you have in your dealership is your people. It is your people that give great buying experiences."

Improve customer experience \- differentiate through service quality

Demonstrate value \- show benefits of franchise system

Engage with associations \- collective defense critical

Educate consumers \- franchise system provides true market competition

Franchise System Defense:

"The franchise system itself represents true market competition. Removing dealers from the equation would limit consumer choice and pricing flexibility, concentrating power with manufacturers rather than expanding competition."

9\. OEM GROWTH, DECLINE, INVENTORY & 2026 STANCE

GM Performance \- Stock Surge

GM stock surged over 55% in 2025 \- leading US-traded automakers

Strong market positioning heading into 2026

EV Market Deterioration \- Critical Shift

Q4 2025 CADSI \- EV Outlook Slumps:

Franchised dealers' future EV sales sentiment: 24 (down from 33 in Q3)

EV leasing sentiment: 27 (down from 36 in Q3)

Most pronounced decline in any category

Root cause: Expiration of federal EV tax credits

Del Grande Dealer Group (DGDG) \- California Reality:

Pre-credit loss: Over 40% of new-vehicle sales were alternative powertrains (EVs \+ plug-in hybrids)

Post-credit loss: Significant decline expected

EV penetration expected to decline further into early 2026 before stabilizing

OEM production constraints: Cannot quickly retool, supply will lag demand shifts

Hybrid Vehicle Surge \- The Fastest Growing Trend

Powertrain Preference Shift:

Hybrid and fuel-efficient vehicles expected to increase significantly

Toyota's long-term hybrid strategy paying dividends

Hybrid models gaining traction across consumer segments

Consumer preference, not government mandates, driving adoption

Market Dynamics:

Hybrids offer fuel efficiency without range anxiety of pure EVs

No tax credit dependency \- more stable demand

Production flexibility \- easier to shift supply than pure EVs

Consumer confidence \- proven technology with established track record

Inventory Availability Concerns

New-vehicle inventory growing but supply/demand mismatches emerging

EV inventory building as demand softens

Hybrid inventory tightening as demand increases

OEM production lag creating supply constraints for high-demand segments

OEM 2026 Stance

Key Themes:

Volume over profitability \- some OEMs pushing vehicles through unconventional channels

Direct sales experimentation \- Scout Motors, others testing DTC models

Powertrain flexibility \- shifting production toward hybrids

Dealer pressure \- OEMs prioritizing volume, creating margin pressure on dealers

Tariff uncertainty \- affecting pricing and production decisions

10\. THE FASTEST GROWING TREND \- HYBRID VEHICLES

Why Hybrids Are Winning

Market Dynamics:

Consumer Confidence: Proven technology with 20+ years of reliability

No Tax Credit Dependency: Stable demand not subject to policy changes

Fuel Efficiency: Addresses consumer concerns about gas prices

Range Advantage: No range anxiety like pure EVs

Production Flexibility: OEMs can quickly shift supply

Price Point: More affordable than pure EVs

Charging Infrastructure: No charging network dependency

Market Evidence:

May 2025 Sales Data: "Hybrid leaders pulling ahead as demand holds steady"

DGDG Experience: Hybrid demand increasing as EV demand softens

Consumer Preference: Shifting toward hybrids and fuel-efficient vehicles

OEM Strategy: Toyota's sustained hybrid investment proving successful

Market Projections

Hybrid Powertrain Market CAGR: 20.8% (through 2035\)

Fastest-growing segment in automotive industry

Expected to accelerate as EV tax credits remain expired

Global trend: Vietnam, other markets seeing hybrid booms

Dealer Implications

Inventory management: Shift stock toward hybrids

Sales training: Educate on hybrid benefits vs. EVs

Service preparation: Hybrid-specific technical training

Marketing messaging: Emphasize reliability, efficiency, no charging concerns
