# VP of Marketing Role Definition
## Small B2B Organization (10–100 Employees)

---

## 1. Core Responsibilities of the VP of Marketing (Hands-On Scope)

### Strategic Ownership (VP Executes Directly)

| Responsibility | VP Action | Cadence |
|----------------|-----------|---------|
| **Go-to-Market Strategy** | Authors GTM plans for new products, market entries, and major campaigns. No delegation—VP writes the strategy document. | Quarterly review, ad-hoc for launches |
| **ICP Definition** | Conducts customer interviews, analyzes win/loss data, defines and refines Ideal Customer Profile. Owns the ICP document. | Quarterly refresh |
| **Positioning & Messaging** | Writes core positioning statements, value propositions, and messaging frameworks. Tests with Sales and customers directly. | Bi-annual overhaul, continuous refinement |
| **Competitive Intelligence** | Maintains competitive positioning matrix. Personally reviews competitor launches, pricing changes, messaging shifts. | Monthly scan, real-time alerts |
| **Annual Marketing Plan** | Authors the full-year marketing plan including budget allocation, channel mix, headcount needs, and KPI targets. | Annually, with quarterly adjustments |

### Direct Execution Responsibilities (VP Hands-on Weekly)

| Task | VP Involvement | Notes |
|------|----------------|-------|
| **Campaign Architecture** | Designs campaign structure: audience segments, offer strategy, channel selection, conversion paths. | VP builds the blueprint; team executes assets |
| **Funnel Design** | Maps full funnel from awareness → MQL → SQL → Opportunity → Closed-Won. Defines stage criteria with Sales. | VP owns funnel definitions; tools configured by team or VP |
| **Tool Selection & Configuration** | Evaluates, selects, and often configures marketing stack (CRM, MAP, analytics, ABM). | VP decides; may delegate implementation |
| **Analytics Interpretation** | Reviews dashboards, identifies anomalies, draws conclusions. Translates data into action. | Weekly review minimum |
| **High-Stakes Content** | Writes or heavily edits cornerstone content: homepage copy, sales decks, investor-facing materials, key landing pages. | As needed; VP is final approver on brand-critical assets |
| **Paid Media Strategy** | Sets budget allocation, channel strategy, audience targeting approach. May manage campaigns directly if no specialist. | Weekly optimization review |
| **Sales Enablement Core Assets** | Creates or directs battlecards, objection handling guides, ROI calculators. | Quarterly refresh cycle |

### Ongoing Operational Responsibilities

| Activity | Frequency | VP Role |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| **Pipeline Impact Monitoring** | Weekly | Reviews marketing-sourced and marketing-influenced pipeline. Adjusts campaigns based on conversion rates. |
| **Campaign Performance Review** | Weekly | Analyzes active campaign metrics. Decides continue/pause/optimize. |
| **Team 1:1s** | Weekly | Direct reports only. Focuses on blockers, priorities, skill development. |
| **Sales-Marketing Sync** | Weekly | Attends or leads. Reviews lead quality feedback, upcoming needs, pipeline status. |
| **Budget Tracking** | Monthly | Reconciles spend against plan. Reallocates based on performance. |
| **Vendor/Agency Management** | Monthly | Reviews deliverables, performance, contract terms. |
| **Board/Investor Prep** | Quarterly | Prepares marketing section of board materials. |

### Time Allocation Expectations

| Timeframe | VP Personal Involvement |
|-----------|------------------------|
| **Weekly** | Campaign review, analytics interpretation, Sales sync, team 1:1s, high-priority content review, pipeline monitoring, tool/process troubleshooting |
| **Monthly** | Budget reconciliation, vendor reviews, content calendar approval, performance reporting to CEO, competitive review |
| **Quarterly** | ICP refresh, messaging audit, GTM planning for next quarter, board prep, OKR/goal setting, team performance reviews |
| **Annually** | Annual plan creation, budget proposal, headcount planning, major tool/vendor decisions, positioning overhaul |

### Task Classification Matrix

#### Must-Be-Done-By-VP

- GTM strategy authorship
- ICP definition and documentation
- Core positioning and messaging
- Budget allocation decisions
- Hiring decisions for marketing roles
- Vendor/agency selection (final decision)
- Crisis communications
- Board/investor materials
- Sales compensation alignment discussions (marketing-sourced vs influenced)
- Funnel stage definitions and SLAs with Sales

#### Can-Be-Delegated (With Clear Briefs)

- Content production (blogs, social, emails)
- Campaign asset creation (ads, landing pages)
- Event logistics
- Social media management
- Basic reporting and dashboard maintenance
- SEO technical execution
- Webinar production
- Email nurture execution
- CRM data hygiene

#### Starts Delegated, Requires VP Review/Intervention

- Campaign performance analysis (team produces, VP interprets)
- Lead scoring model adjustments (team proposes, VP approves)
- New channel experiments (team researches, VP decides)
- Messaging variations for campaigns (team drafts, VP approves)
- Content briefs and outlines (team creates, VP reviews strategic alignment)
- Vendor deliverable acceptance (team manages, VP spot-checks)
- Budget reallocation requests (team flags, VP decides)

## 2. Lean Team Structure and Delegation Model

### Typical Team Composition (10-100 Employee B2B Company)

| Scenario | Team Size | Composition |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| **Minimal (Seed/Series A)** | VP + 0-1 | VP does everything; 1 coordinator/generalist if lucky |
| **Early Growth** | VP + 1-2 | 1 Content/Demand Gen hybrid, 1 Marketing Ops/Coordinator |
| **Scaling** | VP + 3-5 | Content Manager, Demand Gen Manager, Marketing Ops, Designer (often shared or contract) |

### Role Definitions and Ownership

#### Content & Communications Manager (Often Combined with Demand Gen Early On)

**Owned Responsibilities:**
- Blog content production (writing or managing freelancers)
- Social media content calendar and posting
- Email newsletter production
- Case study interviews and drafting
- Webinar content development
- PR pitch list management and outreach execution

**VP Remains Involved:**
- Content strategy and editorial calendar approval
- Messaging alignment review on key pieces
- Case study customer selection
- Thought leadership topic selection

#### Demand Generation Manager (Often Combined with Content Early On)

**Owned Responsibilities:**
- Campaign execution (email, paid, organic)
- Landing page builds (using existing templates)
- A/B test execution
- Lead list building and enrichment
- Webinar/event promotion
- Ad creative variations and testing

**VP Remains Involved:**
- Campaign strategy and target selection
- Budget allocation across channels
- Performance interpretation and optimization decisions
- New channel evaluation

#### Marketing Operations / Coordinator

**Owned Responsibilities:**
- CRM and MAP hygiene
- List uploads and segmentation
- Reporting dashboard maintenance
- Tool administration
- Lead routing configuration
- Sales enablement asset organization

**VP Remains Involved:**
- Lead scoring model design
- Attribution model selection
- Funnel stage definitions
- Tool selection and major configuration decisions

### Commonly Outsourced vs Internal

| Function | Typical Approach | Rationale |
|----------|------------------|-----------|
| **Paid Media Execution** | Outsourced to agency or freelancer | Requires specialized skills; volume doesn't justify FTE |
| **SEO Technical** | Outsourced | Periodic audits, not continuous work |
| **Graphic Design** | Outsourced or shared with Product | Unless high content volume |
| **Video Production** | Outsourced | Capital-intensive, episodic need |
| **PR/Comms** | Outsourced to agency | Relationships and media lists are agency assets |
| **Content Writing** | Hybrid (internal strategy, external production) | Scale through freelancers, keep strategy internal |
| **Website Development** | Outsourced or shared with Engineering | Major builds outsourced; minor edits internal |
| **Marketing Ops/Analytics** | Internal | Core competency; needs institutional knowledge |
| **Demand Gen Strategy** | Internal | Too close to revenue to outsource |
| **Brand/Positioning** | Internal (VP) | Existential; must be owned by company |

### Compensating for Lack of Scale

| Constraint | Compensation Strategy |
|------------|----------------------|
| **No dedicated analyst** | VP builds dashboards personally; uses out-of-box tool reporting; weekly self-serve analysis ritual |
| **No design resource** | Template-based design (Canva, Figma templates); design sprints with contractor; minimize custom creative needs |
| **No dedicated content writer** | Freelance pool for production; VP or SMEs provide outlines; repurpose aggressively (1 asset → 5 formats) |
| **No paid media specialist** | Agency for execution; VP sets strategy and reviews; platform automation for optimization |
| **No marketing ops person** | VP configures tools; uses native integrations; avoids over-complexity; documents everything |
| **Limited budget** | Ruthless prioritization; fewer, better campaigns; heavy organic/content investment; partnership leverage |

## 3. Decision-Making Authority and Escalation Rules

### Decisions VP Makes Independently

| Category | Examples | Threshold |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| **Campaign Execution** | Launch/pause campaigns, A/B tests, channel mix within approved budget | Any campaign within quarterly budget |
| **Content** | Topics, formats, publishing calendar, freelancer assignments | All, unless brand-sensitive |
| **Tool Configuration** | Workflow changes, automation rules, dashboard modifications | Changes within existing tools |
| **Vendor Management** | Day-to-day agency direction, freelancer assignments, deliverable feedback | Within contracted scope |
| **Team Management** | Task assignments, priorities, PTO approval, performance feedback | All direct reports |
| **Budget Reallocation** | Moving budget between campaigns/channels | Up to 20% of quarterly budget |
| **Messaging Variations** | Campaign-level copy, ad variations, email subject lines | Must align with approved positioning |
| **Lead Scoring Adjustments** | Point values, threshold tweaks | Changes that don't affect SQL definition |
| **Event Participation** | Sponsorship decisions | Under $10K typically; check your threshold |

### Decisions Requiring CEO Approval

| Decision Type | Trigger | Process |
|---------------|---------|---------|
| **Annual Budget** | Setting marketing budget for the year | VP proposes, CEO approves |
| **Headcount Changes** | Adding/removing FTEs | VP builds business case, CEO decides |
| **Major Vendor Contracts** | Annual contracts >$25K | VP recommends, CEO signs |
| **Positioning Changes** | Fundamental shifts in how company is positioned | VP proposes with rationale, CEO approves |
| **Pricing Communication** | Any external pricing changes | VP coordinates, CEO approves messaging |
| **New Market Entry** | Geographic or vertical expansion | VP builds GTM, CEO approves |
| **Brand Changes** | Logo, name, visual identity | VP manages process, CEO final say |
| **Major Partnerships** | Co-marketing with significant partners | VP negotiates, CEO approves |
| **Crisis Response** | Public statements on sensitive issues | VP drafts, CEO approves |
| **Budget Overrun** | Exceeding quarterly budget by >10% | Must get approval before spending |

### Decisions Requiring Cross-Functional Alignment

| Decision | Stakeholders | VP Role |
|----------|--------------|---------|
| **ICP Definition/Changes** | Sales, Product, CS | VP leads process, gets buy-in |
| **Lead Scoring Model** | Sales | VP proposes, Sales validates |
| **Funnel Stage Definitions** | Sales | Joint ownership with Sales leader |
| **MQL → SQL Handoff SLA** | Sales | Negotiate and document with Sales |
| **Product Launch Timing** | Product, Sales | VP inputs on market readiness |
| **Pricing Strategy** | Product, Sales, Finance | VP provides market input |
| **Customer Reference Program** | CS, Sales | VP coordinates, CS/Sales nominate |
| **Website Technical Changes** | Engineering/IT | VP prioritizes, Engineering implements |
| **Data/Privacy Compliance** | Legal, IT | VP ensures marketing compliance |
| **Sales Enablement Content** | Sales | VP produces, Sales provides input |

### Escalation Triggers

| Trigger | Action |
|---------|--------|
| **Cost >$25K unbudgeted** | Escalate to CEO |
| **Brand/reputation risk** | Escalate to CEO immediately |
| **Customer data incident** | Escalate to CEO + Legal immediately |
| **Competitive threat (major)** | Inform CEO within 24 hours |
| **Campaign failure (>20% under target)** | Inform CEO in next sync; bring remediation plan |
| **Team conflict (unresolved)** | Escalate to CEO if cross-functional |
| **Vendor non-performance** | CEO awareness if contract termination likely |
| **Legal/compliance concern** | Escalate immediately; pause activity |
| **Sales-Marketing misalignment** | Attempt resolution; escalate if deadlocked |
| **Missed pipeline target** | Proactive CEO communication with analysis |

### Decision Speed Expectations

| Decision Type | Expected Turnaround |
|---------------|---------------------|
| Campaign adjustments | Same day |
| Content approvals | 24-48 hours |
| Budget reallocation (<20%) | 48 hours |
| Vendor issues | 24 hours to acknowledge, 1 week to resolve |
| CEO-required decisions | 1 week (VP prepares materials within 48 hours) |
| Cross-functional alignment | 1-2 weeks depending on complexity |

## 5. Upward Communication and CEO Interface

### Metrics Reported to CEO

#### Primary Metrics (Always Reported)

| Metric | Definition | Target-Setting | Reporting Frequency |
|--------|------------|----------------|---------------------|
| **Marketing-Sourced Pipeline** | $ value of opportunities where first touch was marketing | Set annually, tracked monthly | Weekly snapshot, monthly deep-dive |
| **Marketing-Influenced Pipeline** | $ value of opportunities with any marketing touch | Set annually, tracked monthly | Monthly |
| **MQLs Generated** | Leads meeting qualification criteria | Set quarterly | Weekly |
| **MQL → SQL Conversion Rate** | % of MQLs accepted by Sales | Baseline established, improve over time | Monthly |
| **SQL → Opportunity Conversion Rate** | % of SQLs becoming opportunities | Baseline established, improve over time | Monthly |
| **Cost per MQL** | Total marketing spend / MQLs | Benchmark against industry; improve over time | Monthly |
| **Pipeline Velocity** | Average days from MQL to Closed-Won | Baseline established, reduce over time | Quarterly |
| **Marketing ROI** | (Revenue from marketing-sourced deals - Marketing spend) / Marketing spend | >3x for B2B typically | Quarterly |

#### Secondary Metrics (Reported Monthly or As Relevant)

| Metric | When Reported |
|--------|---------------|
| Website traffic and conversion rates | Monthly |
| Email engagement (open rates, CTR) | Monthly if issues arise |
| Content performance (downloads, engagement) | Quarterly |
| Paid media efficiency (ROAS, CPC, CPL) | Monthly |
| Brand awareness (if measured) | Quarterly or bi-annually |
| NPS/customer sentiment (if marketing owns) | Quarterly |

### Reporting Cadence and Format

| Touchpoint | Frequency | Format | Content |
|------------|-----------|--------|---------|
| **Weekly Sync** | Weekly (30 min) | Verbal + 1-page summary | Pipeline snapshot, active campaigns, blockers, decisions needed |
| **Monthly Report** | Monthly | 3-5 page doc or slides | Full metrics review, campaign performance, budget status, next month priorities |
| **Quarterly Business Review** | Quarterly | 10-15 slides | Quarter results vs goals, wins/losses analysis, next quarter plan, resource requests |
| **Board Prep** | Per board meeting | 3-5 slides | High-level metrics, strategic initiatives, market insights |
| **Ad-Hoc** | As needed | Slack/email | Urgent issues, quick wins, competitive alerts |

### Tying Marketing to Revenue

#### Attribution Model (Recommended for Small B2B)

**Primary Model:** First-touch attribution for marketing-sourced pipeline
- If marketing was the first known touch, marketing gets credit for the opportunity

**Secondary Model:** Multi-touch influence for marketing-influenced pipeline
- Any opportunity with 2+ marketing touches counts as influenced

**Reporting Construct:**
```
Total Pipeline This Quarter: $X
├── Marketing-Sourced: $Y (Z%)
├── Marketing-Influenced: $W (V%)
├── Sales-Sourced: $A (B%)
└── Partner-Sourced: $C (D%)

Closed-Won Revenue This Quarter: $X
├── Marketing-Sourced: $Y (Z%)
└── Marketing-Influenced: $W (V%)
```

### Communicating Risks, Failures, and Experiments

#### Risk Communication Framework

| Risk Level | Communication Approach |
|------------|------------------------|
| **Low** | Mention in weekly sync, no escalation |
| **Medium** | Dedicated section in weekly sync, written summary |
| **High** | Immediate Slack/call, followed by written brief with mitigation plan |
| **Critical** | Immediate call, pause affected activities, written incident report within 24 hours |

#### Failure Communication Template

```
What happened: [Factual description]
Impact: [Pipeline, spend, brand, timeline affected]
Root cause: [Why it happened]
What we're doing: [Immediate actions]
What we'll do differently: [Prevention]
Ask: [What you need from CEO, if anything]
```

#### Experiment Communication

**Before Launch:**
- Hypothesis, test design, success criteria, downside risk, investment required
- CEO approval if spend >$5K or brand risk exists

**During:**
- Brief status in weekly sync

**After:**
- Results, learnings, recommendation (scale, iterate, kill)
- No blame for failed experiments that were well-designed

### CEO Communication Principles

1. **No surprises.** CEO should hear bad news from you first, not from Sales, the board, or customers.

2. **Lead with the number.** Start with the metric, then explain.

3. **Own the miss.** If targets are missed, say so clearly. Bring analysis and remediation.

4. **Bring solutions, not just problems.** Every problem presented should have at least one proposed solution.

5. **Be concise.** Respect CEO time. Bottom-line up front, details available if wanted.

6. **Quantify confidence.** "I'm 80% confident we'll hit pipeline target" is more useful than "I think we're on track."

7. **Flag dependencies.** If marketing success depends on Sales, Product, or budget decisions, make it explicit.

## 6. Operating Model and End-to-End Workflow

### How Initiatives Are Identified and Prioritized

#### Initiative Sources

| Source | Examples | Frequency |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| **Annual Planning** | Major campaigns, launches, channel investments | Annually |
| **Quarterly Planning** | Next quarter priorities based on pipeline needs | Quarterly |
| **Sales Feedback** | Content gaps, competitive issues, lead quality concerns | Ongoing (weekly sync) |
| **Customer Feedback** | Messaging resonance, content requests | Ongoing (CS input, win/loss) |
| **Competitive Moves** | Respond to competitor campaigns, positioning shifts | As they happen |
| **Performance Data** | Double down on what's working, cut what's not | Weekly review |
| **CEO/Leadership** | Strategic priorities, board feedback | As communicated |
| **Opportunistic** | Partnership opportunities, PR moments, events | As they arise |

#### Prioritization Framework (ICE Scoring Adapted)

For each potential initiative, score 1-10:

| Factor | Question |
|--------|----------|
| **Impact** | How much pipeline/revenue could this drive? |
| **Confidence** | How certain are we this will work? (Data, past results, market evidence) |
| **Ease** | How quickly can we execute with available resources? |

**Score = (Impact × Confidence × Ease) / 10**

**Prioritization Rules:**
1. Score all initiatives
2. Rank by score
3. Apply constraints: budget, headcount, dependencies
4. Top initiatives become quarterly commitments
5. Remaining become backlog for opportunistic execution

### Campaign Workflow: Idea → Execution → Measurement

#### Phase 1: Ideation & Approval (1-2 weeks)

```
Trigger (Sales need, data insight, strategic priority)
    ↓
VP drafts Campaign Brief
- Objective (pipeline target, awareness goal)
- Target audience (ICP segment)
- Offer/hook
- Channels
- Timeline
- Budget
- Success metrics
    ↓
VP reviews with team for feasibility
    ↓
If >$10K or strategic: CEO approval
    ↓
Campaign enters execution queue
```

#### Phase 2: Planning & Production (2-4 weeks)

```
Team breaks campaign into tasks
    ↓
Asset list created:
- Landing page(s)
- Email sequence
- Ad creative
- Content pieces
- Sales enablement
    ↓
Assignments made (internal, freelance, agency)
    ↓
VP reviews key assets at 50% and 90% complete
    ↓
QA and testing
    ↓
Launch checklist completed
```

#### Phase 3: Execution (Ongoing)

```
Campaign launches
    ↓
Daily: Team monitors basic metrics (spend, clicks, form fills)
    ↓
Weekly: VP reviews performance dashboard
- Traffic and conversion by channel
- CPL and spend pacing
- Lead quality (Sales feedback)
    ↓
VP decides: Continue, optimize, pause, or kill
    ↓
Optimization cycle (weekly):
- A/B tests
- Budget reallocation
- Messaging refinements
- Audience adjustments
```

#### Phase 4: Measurement & Learning (At campaign end + ongoing attribution)

```
Campaign ends or reaches steady state
    ↓
Team produces Campaign Report:
- Spend vs budget
- Leads generated
- Pipeline created
- Cost per lead, cost per opportunity
- Top performing channels/assets
- What worked, what didn't
    ↓
VP analyzes and adds strategic interpretation
    ↓
Learnings documented and shared
    ↓
Feeds into next campaign planning
    ↓
Attribution continues (track to closed-won over sales cycle)
```

### Incorporating Feedback from Sales and Customers

#### Sales Feedback Loop

| Mechanism | Frequency | Action |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| **Weekly Sales-Marketing Sync** | Weekly | Review lead quality, discuss upcoming needs, share wins/losses |
| **Lead Quality Survey** | Per lead or weekly batch | Sales rates lead quality; marketing adjusts targeting |
| **Win/Loss Interviews** | Monthly (or per significant deal) | Understand what resonated, what didn't; feed into messaging |
| **Pipeline Review Attendance** | Weekly or bi-weekly | VP attends Sales pipeline review to understand deal context |
| **Slack Channel** | Ongoing | Real-time feedback, competitive sightings, content requests |

**Feedback Integration Process:**
```
Feedback received (e.g., "Leads from LinkedIn aren't qualified")
    ↓
VP investigates (check data, talk to SDRs, review lead sources)
    ↓
Root cause identified (e.g., targeting too broad, wrong offer)
    ↓
Adjustment made (tighten targeting, change offer, pause channel)
    ↓
Communicate change back to Sales
    ↓
Monitor for improvement (2-4 weeks)
    ↓
Report outcome in next sync
```

#### Customer Feedback Loop

| Mechanism | Frequency | Action |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| **Customer Interviews** | Quarterly (5-10 customers) | VP or team conducts; informs ICP, messaging |
| **Win/Loss Analysis** | Ongoing | Understand buying journey, competitive factors |
| **Support Ticket Themes** | Monthly review | Identify content gaps, objection themes |
| **NPS/Survey Comments** | Per survey | Extract messaging and positioning insights |
| **Sales Call Recordings** | Ongoing (sample) | Understand customer language, objections |

### Correcting or Killing Underperforming Initiatives

#### Performance Thresholds

| Metric | Yellow Flag (Investigate) | Red Flag (Act Immediately) |
|--------|---------------------------|----------------------------|
| **CPL** | >50% above target | >100% above target |
| **Conversion Rate** | >30% below benchmark | >50% below benchmark |
| **Pipeline Created** | <70% of target at midpoint | <50% of target at midpoint |
| **Lead Quality Score** | <3/5 average from Sales | <2/5 average from Sales |
| **Email Engagement** | <15% open rate, <1% CTR | <10% open rate, <0.5% CTR |
| **Paid Media ROAS** | <2x | <1x |

#### Decision Framework

```
Performance reviewed (weekly)
    ↓
Underperformance detected
    ↓
Diagnose root cause:
- Targeting issue?
- Offer/message issue?
- Channel issue?
- Timing issue?
- Execution issue?
    ↓
Determine fixability:
- Quick fix possible? → Optimize and continue
- Fundamental flaw? → Pause or kill
- Need more data? → Set deadline for decision (2 weeks max)
    ↓
If killing:
- Document learnings
- Communicate to stakeholders
- Reallocate budget
- Update backlog
    ↓
If optimizing:
- Implement changes
- Set new checkpoint (1-2 weeks)
- If still underperforming, escalate to kill decision
```

#### Kill Criteria (Any One Triggers Kill Decision)

1. **Spend >50% of budget with <25% of target pipeline**
2. **3 consecutive weeks of declining performance despite optimization**
3. **Sales feedback consistently negative (<2/5 quality) with no improvement**
4. **Strategic misalignment discovered (market changed, product changed)**
5. **Opportunity cost too high (better use of resources identified)**

### Weekly Operating Rhythm

| Day | Activity | Participants | Output |
|-----|----------|--------------|--------|
| **Monday** | Team standup: week priorities, blockers | VP + Team | Aligned weekly plan |
| **Monday** | Campaign performance review | VP (solo or with team) | Optimization decisions |
| **Tuesday** | Sales-Marketing sync | VP + Sales Leader + SDR lead | Feedback actioned, alignment confirmed |
| **Wednesday** | Content/creative review | VP + Content lead | Assets approved or redirected |
| **Thursday** | 1:1s with direct reports | VP + each report | Blockers cleared, development discussed |
| **Friday** | CEO sync (if weekly) | VP + CEO | Status communicated, decisions made |
| **Friday** | Week retrospective | VP (solo) | Learnings captured, next week prepped |
