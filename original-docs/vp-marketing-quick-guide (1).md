# VP of Marketing — Quick Guide
### Small B2B (10-100 employees) | Player-Coach Role

---

## You Own These 5 Things

| # | What | Translation |
|---|------|-------------|
| **1** | Strategy | You write the plan. No one else. |
| **2** | Pipeline | Marketing's job = feed Sales with qualified leads |
| **3** | Message | What we say, how we say it, to whom |
| **4** | Budget | Where money goes, what it returns |
| **5** | Team Output | Small team, you're in the weeds with them |

## What YOU Do vs What TEAM Does

### You Do (Non-Negotiable)

- Write the GTM strategy
- Define who we sell to (ICP)
- Own positioning & messaging
- Decide budget allocation
- Interpret data → make calls
- High-stakes content (homepage, sales deck)
- Talk to CEO, board, Sales leader

### Team Does (You Review)

- Blog posts, social, emails
- Campaign execution
- Landing pages
- Ad creative
- CRM hygiene
- Event logistics

### Outsource

- Paid media execution (agency)
- Design (freelancer)
- PR (agency)
- SEO audits (consultant)
- Overflow writing (freelancers)

## When Things Go Wrong

### Yellow Flags (Investigate)

- CPL 50%+ over target
- Conversion 30%+ below normal
- Sales says "leads suck" 2 weeks in a row

### Red Flags (Act Now)

- Spending money, getting nothing
- Sales stops following up on leads
- Major competitor move you missed

### How to Communicate Bad News

```
1. What happened (facts only)
2. Impact (pipeline, money, time)
3. Why it happened
4. What you're doing about it
5. What you need (if anything)
```

**Rule:** CEO hears it from you first. No surprises.
