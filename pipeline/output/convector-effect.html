What Is the Convector Effect?

Convection in Physics

A convector heats air, which rises, cools, falls back down, and gets reheated — creating a continuous circular current that produces constant motion but no net displacement. The air is always moving. Energy is always being consumed. But the room only gets warm if the cycle runs long enough without interruption.

Convection in Strolid's Marketing

Vin injects energy (a new directive). The team heats up (begins execution). Before the work can reach the market and produce results, Vin changes direction (the air cools). The team falls back to baseline and the cycle restarts. The result: constant activity, constant energy expenditure, zero forward progress on leads.

Unlike a real convector that eventually warms the room, Strolid's cycle never completes because the interval between pivots (avg 14 days) is shorter than the minimum time for any marketing initiative to produce measurable results (60-90 days).

The Convector Effect Summary

A convector produces constant air circulation without ever opening a window. Strolid's marketing produces constant activity without ever completing a campaign. The minimum time for any marketing initiative to produce measurable ROI is 60–90 days. Vin's average pivot interval is 14 days — 6.4x faster than results can appear. The system is designed to fail. Not by malice, but by a mental model that treats marketing as a series of isolated directives rather than a compounding investment that requires patience, consistency, and measurement. After 6 months of the convector effect: zero measurable lead increase, Sophia engagement declined (15% → 11%), 67 abandoned action items, and marketing sentiment at 0% positive.
