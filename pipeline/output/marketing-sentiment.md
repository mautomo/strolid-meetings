## Conclusions & Recommendations

### Key Findings

1. **Marketing Sentiment is Primarily Mixed (46.5%)** - This indicates ongoing tension between ambition and execution, with teams struggling to align despite clear strategic intent.

2. **Declining Positive Sentiment Over Time** - The shift from neutral/status-quo meetings in fall 2025 to predominantly mixed sentiment by Q1 2026 suggests execution challenges are accumulating.

3. **Disagreements Over Conflicting Priorities is the #1 Frustration** - 13 of 43 meetings (30%) mention disagreements/conflicting priorities, suggesting unclear prioritization or resource allocation.

4. **Leadership Sentiment Imbalance** - Michael Donovan, Joe Furnari, and Jason Branham show patterns of appearing in negative meetings far more than positive ones (9N:2P, 6N:2P, 6N:1P respectively).

5. **Very Few Clearly Positive Meetings (4.7%)** - Only 2 meetings show unambiguously positive sentiment, suggesting that celebration of wins and clear progress communication is insufficient.

### Recommended Actions

1. **Clarify Marketing Priorities and Roadmap** - The #1 frustration is conflicting priorities. Establish a clear, agreed-upon prioritization framework.

2. **Improve Execution Transparency** - Track and communicate progress on key initiatives more visibly to shift from mixed sentiment toward positive.

3. **Address Michael Donovan's Concerns** - As the most frequently negative contributor, consider 1:1 conversations to understand specific blockers and frustrations.

4. **Increase Celebration of Wins** - With only 4.7% clearly positive meetings, create dedicated forums for celebrating delivered work and momentum.

5. **Monthly Sentiment Check-ins** - Institute monthly retrospectives focused on what went well vs. what frustrated the team to course-correct faster.
